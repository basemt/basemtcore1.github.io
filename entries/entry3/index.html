Anyways, seeing as I am in fact studying Communication Design (which you may or may not have already known I can't really tell who you are through the screen your camera's a bit too blurry), I think it's best to get this category out of the way while I still have your attention (at least I hope I do). I recently had a typography assignment in one of my classes where we had to choose a font to present in class. This led me down a whirlpool of words in all sorts of shapes and strokes and sizes. So, in spirit of being a word nerd this entire week, here are some of my fav and not-so fav fonts:

Henny Penny

A fav, feels like something you'd see in a late 90's/early 00's TV show title sequence.

Faster One

A not-so fav, I can see what they were going for but it isn't giving the intended effect justice.

Creepster

A not-so fav, with Halloween coming up I'd rather something with a bit more character to it than just an oozing effect.

Griffy

A fav, this is what Halloween needs. It kinda reminds me of those Tim Burton movies.

Alumni Sans Collegiate One

Definitiely a not-so fav. My eyes started to hurt after a while from trying to read this.

Barriecito

A fav, something about the contrast throughout makes me think of ransom letters in movies and true crime docs.

Kavoon

A fav for sure. Not sure how to put it into words besides that it's bubbly, but in a good way.

Fredericka The Great

No. Just... no. Not-so fav and the name is enough justification besides the obvious cringeyness to it.

Rubik Moonrocks

Another not-so fav. This reminds me of pimples or warts which shouldn't be something you'd find on letters.

Parisienne

A fav for the nostalgic vibe it has, I can imagine it being used in old black and white movies to say "The End".

Libre Barcode

This definitely would've been used in a scrappy 2014 tumblr edit... not-so fav.

Unifraktur Maguntia

Last but certainly not least, a fav for its gothic style. Another one with slight Halloween-ish tendencies, but mainly vampires/dracula to me.





Well, that's it for now. I hope you enjoyed looking at these fonts as much as I did trying to code them in! One of the main things I'd say that I learnt from all of this is that I will in fact judge a book by it's cover, especially if any of the not-so favs mentioned here were intentionally used as a stylistic choice.
